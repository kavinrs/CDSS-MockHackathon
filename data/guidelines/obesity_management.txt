Obesity Management and Weight Loss Guidelines

ASSESSMENT AND CLASSIFICATION
Obesity is defined as excessive body fat accumulation that presents health risks. Body Mass Index (BMI) serves as the primary classification tool: normal weight (18.5-24.9 kg/m²), overweight (25.0-29.9 kg/m²), class I obesity (30.0-34.9 kg/m²), class II obesity (35.0-39.9 kg/m²), and class III obesity (40.0 kg/m² or greater). Waist circumference measurement provides additional risk stratification, with increased cardiometabolic risk at waist circumferences exceeding 102cm in men or 88cm in women. Comprehensive assessment should include evaluation of obesity-related complications such as type 2 diabetes, hypertension, dyslipidemia, obstructive sleep apnea, and osteoarthritis.

TREATMENT GOALS
Initial weight loss goals should target 5-10% reduction from baseline body weight over 6 months, which provides clinically meaningful improvements in cardiovascular risk factors, glucose metabolism, and blood pressure. Sustained weight loss maintenance requires ongoing behavioral support and lifestyle modifications. For patients with BMI 30 kg/m² or greater, or BMI 27 kg/m² or greater with obesity-related complications, more intensive interventions including pharmacotherapy or bariatric surgery may be appropriate.

DIETARY INTERVENTIONS
Caloric restriction forms the cornerstone of weight loss therapy. A deficit of 500-750 kcal daily typically results in weight loss of 0.5-1.0 kg per week. Multiple dietary approaches demonstrate efficacy, including reduced-calorie diets, low-carbohydrate diets, Mediterranean diets, and intermittent fasting protocols. Dietary composition should emphasize nutrient-dense foods including vegetables, fruits, whole grains, lean proteins, and healthy fats while limiting added sugars, refined carbohydrates, and saturated fats. Portion control, mindful eating practices, and regular meal patterns support sustainable weight management.

PHYSICAL ACTIVITY
Regular physical activity is essential for weight loss maintenance and provides independent health benefits. Initial recommendations include 150 minutes weekly of moderate-intensity aerobic activity, gradually progressing to 300 minutes weekly for enhanced weight loss and prevention of weight regain. Resistance training at least two days weekly preserves lean muscle mass during weight loss and improves metabolic health. Reducing sedentary behavior and increasing daily movement through lifestyle activities contributes to energy expenditure and overall health.

BEHAVIORAL THERAPY AND PHARMACOTHERAPY
Behavioral interventions addressing eating patterns, physical activity, and cognitive strategies improve weight loss outcomes. Self-monitoring of food intake, body weight, and physical activity enhances awareness and accountability. FDA-approved weight loss medications including phentermine-topiramate, naltrexone-bupropion, liraglutide, and semaglutide can augment lifestyle interventions in appropriate candidates. Bariatric surgery, including Roux-en-Y gastric bypass and sleeve gastrectomy, is indicated for patients with BMI 40 kg/m² or greater, or BMI 35 kg/m² or greater with obesity-related complications, providing substantial and sustained weight loss with improvement in comorbidities.
